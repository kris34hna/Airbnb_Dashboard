### Global Airbnb Performance Dashboard

Analyzing Airbnb's global presence across 10 major cities to uncover market trends, pricing strategies, customer satisfaction patterns, and host trust signals — helping stakeholders make smarter business and travel decisions.


## Brief Summary
An interactive Power BI dashboard analyzing 2,79,712 Airbnb listings across 10 global cities — covering growth trends, market share, property pricing, ratings, seasonality, review behavior, and host verification — to answer: "Where should Airbnb focus its growth, regulation, and marketing efforts?"

## Overview
This project dives deep into Airbnb's global dataset to surface insights for three key stakeholders — business strategists, hosts, and travelers. Using Power BI, the dashboard is structured across multiple analytical sections that answer real business questions around market dominance, pricing logic, customer satisfaction, seasonal demand, and platform trust.

## Problem Statement
Airbnb operates across hundreds of global cities with millions of listings. Understanding where listings are concentrated, how pricing varies by property type, when demand peaks, and how trustworthy hosts are is critical for growth and platform integrity.
Core Business Questions:


"Where should Airbnb focus its growth, regulation, or marketing efforts?"
"Where is Airbnb doing well and where is it failing customers?"
"Can I trust Airbnb hosts?"

| Tool |  Purpose|

## Methods

1.Data Cleaning (Power Query) — Handled nulls, standardized city names, formatted date columns, and created derived fields for property type grouping and host verification flags.

2.DAX Measures — Built calculated KPIs including cumulative market share %, average ratings by city, review frequency distribution, and seasonal demand by month.

3.Dashboard Design (Power BI) — Structured into focused analytical sections with consistent Airbnb branding (red/white/green theme), interactive filters, and drill-through capability.


## Key Insights

1. Paris dominates with 23.13% of total listings; top 3 cities (Paris, New York, Sydney) control nearly 50% of all supply.  
2. Airbnb listings peaked around 2015–2016 (Maturity phase) before declining — COVID-19 caused a sharp drop post-2019 with signs of reinvention.  
3. Hotel rooms are the most expensive property type at $800 avg, followed by Entire place ($673), Shared room ($580), and Private room ($462).  
4. Mexico City and Rio de Janeiro have the highest avg ratings (95), while Hong Kong and Istanbul rank lower (90–91).  
5. Seasonality varies by city — Paris and Rome peak in summer months, while New York shows more consistent year-round demand.  
6. Review frequency analysis reveals most reviews come from one-time users, with very few power users writing multiple reviews — skewing engagement metrics.  
7. Host verification is a critical trust signal — a significant portion of hosts lack full verification, raising platform safety concerns.  
8. Supply is highly concentrated — top cities drive the majority of listings, indicating Airbnb's growth is not evenly distributed globally.  


## Dashboard / Output

The Power BI dashboard is organized into 3 pages:  
Page 1 — Big Picture Overview  
KPI Cards: Total Listings (2,79,712) · Cities (10) · Hosts (1,82,024) · Property Types (144) · Reviews (5,373K)  
Visuals: New Listings Growth Timeline (2008–2021) with lifecycle phases — Introduction → Growth → Maturity → Decline → Reinvention → COVID-19  

Page 2 — Market Share & Pricing    
Visuals: Market Share by City (Pareto chart with cumulative %) · Superhost vs Non-Superhost Listings · Avg Price by Property Type · Avg Rating by City · Detail   Level toggle (Overall vs Detailed Rating)  

Page 3 — Seasonality Analysis  
Visuals: % of Monthly Reviews by City (Mexico City, New York, Paris, Rome, Sydney) — stacked area chart showing seasonal demand patterns across all 12 months  

## Results & Conclusion  
The analysis reveals that Airbnb's supply is heavily concentrated in a few global cities, with Paris, New York, and Sydney driving nearly half the listings. Pricing data confirms that hotel rooms cost significantly more than Airbnb alternatives, justifying traveler preference in high-cost cities. Seasonal patterns vary significantly by geography, and host verification remains a platform integrity challenge. The growth timeline clearly shows Airbnb's business lifecycle — from rapid growth pre-2016 to COVID-19 impact and early signs of recovery.
